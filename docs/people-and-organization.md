# People & Organization

## Organization (Singleton)

The Organization document controls site-wide branding — the name, logos, mission statement, and legal text that appear across the entire website. It is a **singleton**, meaning there is exactly one instance. You can edit it but cannot create a second one or delete it.

The organization's logo and slogan are displayed on the homepage hero. Logos, copyright, nonprofit notice, and small print appear in the site footer.

## People

Person documents represent team members. How a person appears on the website depends on whether they have a Personal Story:

- **With a Personal Story** — The person gets a dedicated page at `/about/{name}` and a "Read More" link from the team overview. Personal stories should be written in **first person** (e.g., "I became involved in grief support when...").
- **Without a Personal Story** — The person appears on the team overview page only, with their avatar, role, short bio, and social links. No dedicated page is created.

Short bios should be written in **third person** (e.g., "Jane is a grief counselor...").

## Person Groups

Person Groups organize people into named collections, such as "Board of Directors" or "Advisory Council."

On the About page, the core team Person Group is displayed with each member's avatar, role, short bio, and social links. The **order of members in the Studio list determines their display order** on the website — drag members to reorder them.
